the challenge

People with limited motor function want to look good too. Adaptive clothing that is available largely in the market, is very basic and plain.

Project Goal

The goal is to come up with an app that brings all fashionable adaptive clothing to one place, making it easier for the users to explore them.

user research

An important step in empathizing with the users was listening to their voices and their challenges. I watched several interviews with young people who have partial paralysis, or multiple sclerosis, or even cerebral palsy. It was humbling to see these warriors perform their daily tasks. The things that most people do with ease without giving a conscious thought to it, can take hours for people facing mobility challenges. With users who shop for adaptive clothing, it was evident that they felt they lacked choices. There was a clear desire to be able to find adaptive clothing that made them happy and feel confident. They wanted to be able to exercise some independence when it came to choosing and wearing clothes that they liked.

user persona

Persona 1

Sarah

"I love being fashionable and look good just like everyone else my age."

Sarah is a young and vibrant individual who was diagnosed with Acute Flaccid Myelitis(AFM), a rare neurologic condition that left her partially paralysed in shoulders and one arm. She loves her independence and is very social with a large group of friends. She wants to put her best foot forward while looking smart and polished.

Problem statement

Sarah is a high school student with disability who needs a one-stop shop for fashionable adaptive clothing because she wants to look good without navigating multiple sites with her limited motor function.

Frustrations

Persona 2

Emma

"I choose to stay positive. Looking good makes me feel good."

Emma is a community advisor with her district. She moves around a lot and meets new people everyday. She enjoys interacting with them and likes to look her best when she is out. Emma was diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis(M.S.) at the age of 40. Her disease is progressive and the tremors make it difficult to use her hands for everyday activities. She stays optimistic and finds ways to adapt to her new situation. She is open to make the changes that will enable her to be independent and free.

starting the design

I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the research phase. My focus was on discoverability of good clothing options and ease of use.

digital wireframes

After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Accio Clothing app. These designs focused on delivering personalised suggestions to users of different abilities to make their own clothing choices.

low-fidelity prototype

To prepare for usability testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype that connected the userflow of creating a unique profile and receiving clothing suggestions based off it. Link to Accios Low-fidelity prototype.

Mockups

Native app

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes like a search bar with audio input and a navigation bar with larger clickable areas and accompanying text.

Additional design changes included making the dropdown menus larger and hence more accessible.

Here is a link to the high-fidelity prototype.

responsive designs

The designs for screen size variation included mobile, tablet and desktop. I optimized the designs to meet specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Accessibility considerations

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers.

The 'Create the profile' feature reduces the amount of work someone has to put in every time they visit the site.

Website is designed to be keyboard accessible.

impact

Users shared they felt like this was their shopping mall with great brands and great designs. This filled a gap for them.

What i learned

Designing in dark mode helped me explore some new design concepts. Designing for accessibility was a great learning experience.

next steps

I would design more screens across all devices to explore more user flows.

I would add micro interactions and animations for screens.

I would make the app more accessible.
