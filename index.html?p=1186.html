project overview

Millions of products are sold on Amazon. Sellers upload these products through the Amazon Seller portal. This project is a redesign of the Amazon Seller Portal, to enable small businesses like Islander Clothing Co. to list their products more easily by making the process more user-friendly and intuitive.

the challenge

Small business owners who want to sell on Amazon, find it difficult to list the products themselves. The seller portal and product upload feature are somewhat text heavy, with no clear hierarchy. The requirements are not conveyed in a simple and user-friendly way. The seller often looks for outside help in uploading products.

Project Goal

Redesign the Amazon Seller Portal to enable small business owners to easily understand the process and requirements, and hence upload their products in a fast and easy way.

user research

During my work as an E-Commerce Coordinator with Islander Clothing Co.(name changed for anonymity), I had the opportunity to closely observe the interaction between the seller and the Amazon Seller Portal. I was “The Fly on the Wall” and observed the users closely. While they were excited about the prospects of online selling, they were struggling to gather requirements, make product listings and upload images that met Amazon guidelines. The whole experience for them turned out to be very overwhelming and time consuming. The user felt frustrated and their product launch was delayed. The user considered abandoning the process to explore other marketplaces. This is a huge opportunity for improvement to meet the users where they are.

Users' pain points

Time Consuming

The product upload process requires a lot of forms and details upfront, which can be time consuming and overwhelming for the user.

Navigation

The portal is quite text heavy, with a lot of information for the seller to process at one time.

Image Requirements

Amazon requires sellers to post images with a white background. Users find this particularly painful as there is no in-built feature to help with this.

user persona

James

"It is so difficult to list my products. I am never sure whether they satisfy the requirements."

James owns a small clothing business and would like to grow his sales by selling on platforms like Amazon. But James is not tech savvy and finds it difficult to conform to various regulations and requirements mandated by the platform. He often runs to family and friends for help and finds it frustrating. He wishes he could manage it all by himself.

Findings

refining the design

mockups

Based on the insights from the usability study, I made changes to make the “Add new products” feature more prominent by bringing it higher up on the homepage.This allowed easier discovery of the feature without the need to scroll down much and avoid confusion.

To help with category selection, I added a dropdown menu button to select from a pre-existing list. I also explained what the terminology meant in the field boxes, to assist in form filling.

I included considerations for additional screen sizes in my mockups based on my earlier wireframes. Because sellers have access to a variety of devices, I felt it was important to optimize the seller experience for a range of device sizes, such as mobile and desktop so users have the smoothest experience possible.

Accessibility considerations

I used headings with different sized text for clear visual hierarchy

I used landmarks to help users navigate the site, including users who rely on assistive technologies

impact

Our target users shared that the design was intuitive to navigate through, more engaging with the images, and demonstrated a clear visual hierarchy.

What i learned

I learnt that presenting information in a usable and intuitive manner, that helps users accomplish their tasks effortlessly is highly valuable. I will strive to make rest of the navigation and features of the site more usable, and incorporate feedback at all levels.

next steps

I would add micro interactions to make the prototype more realistic.

I would add more functionality into the prototype, to demonstrate other features as well.

I would make the site more accessible.
